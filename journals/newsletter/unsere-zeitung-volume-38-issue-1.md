American Historical Society of Germans from Russia ## Unsere ZeitungVolume 38, Issue 1 — Maifest: The German Celebration of Spring May 1, 2026 ## Volume 38, Issue 1 — Maifest: The German Celebration of Spring## May 1, 2026Dear Denver Metro Chapter Members and Friends, It has been a long time since many of us sat in a room together to share food, stories, and our Germans-from-Russia heritage. The last few years have been hard on in‑person groups — COVID, rising venue costs, and changing health needs all took a toll on our ability to meet. Over the past months, a small group of volunteers has been talking, listening, and looking for a simpler, more affordable way forward for the Denver Metro Chapter. We have been guided by the lessons of our past: our chapter thrived when we gathered around food in accessible spaces, and it struggled when venues became too expensive or too hard for older members to navigate. "It doesn't matter if we've got 20 members or 200 members. We just need people that are enthusiastic and want to keep their heritage going." — Shirley Wilcoxon, Board President ## Maifest: Why We Chose Spring to Re‑GatherIn many German communities, Maifest (May Day) marks the arrival of spring and a fresh start for the year's work and celebrations. It is a time to come together, decorate the Maypole, and enjoy music, food, and fellowship. For Germans from Russia and their descendants, spring has often meant both hard work and community life — gardens, fields, church gatherings, and village events that helped people endure difficult times together. This year, we are choosing Maifest as our symbol for the Denver Metro Chapter: a season of new beginnings, careful steps, and a renewed commitment to our shared history. Maifest has always been about a community choosing to begin again. That is what we are doing together in 2026. ## Spring Maifest Potluck — Save the Date

▶ The history behind the bonfires and ribbons: ## Recipe: Westphalian Ham on Potato Pancakes (Maifest Style)
## Ingredients (4–6 servings)- 8–10 potato pancakes (homemade or store-bought)
- 8–10 thin slices Westphalian or Black Forest ham (or any good smoked ham)
- 1 cup sour cream
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: chopped green onion for garnish
## Instructions- Stir together sour cream, parsley, salt, and pepper. Adjust seasoning to taste.
- Cook potato pancakes until golden and crisp on both sides.
- Place a warm pancake on each plate. Top with parsley sour cream and a slice of ham.
- Garnish with extra parsley or green onion if desired.
- Serve immediately while pancakes are hot and crisp.
