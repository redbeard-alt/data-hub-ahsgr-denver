## Clues

- **MAIBAUM** – The traditional German name for the Maypole.
- **MAYPOLE** – A towering, decorated pole symbolizing new life and community unity.
- **WALPURGISNACHT** – The legendary night of April 30 when witches are said to gather and welcome spring.
- **HEXENNACHT** – German for "Witches' Night."
- **OSTARA** – An ancient Germanic goddess of dawn, light, and spring awakening.
- **BELTANE** – A Celtic festival celebrating the return of the sun and the land's fertility.
- **MAIBOWLE** – A refreshing May punch made with wine, champagne, and sweet woodruff.
- **MAIBOCK** – A strong spring beer brewed especially for May Day.
- **WOODRUFF** – The sweet herb that gives Maibowle its distinct flavor.
- **BROCKEN** – The highest peak in the Harz Mountains, where witches supposedly gather.
- **BONFIRE** – Lit on the eve of May Day to ward off winter spirits.
- **RIBBONS** – Colorful decorations woven around the Maypole in traditional dances.
- **DIRNDL** – Traditional Bavarian dress worn by women at Maifest.
- **LEDERHOSEN** – Traditional leather trousers worn by men during May festivities.
- **BAVARIA** – Southern German region famous for its elaborate Maypole traditions.
- **BLINI** – Yeast-leavened Russian buckwheat pancakes celebrating the sun's return.
- **EQUINOX** – When day and night are equal, marking the official arrival of spring.
- **FERTILITY** – A central theme of spring festivals, symbolized by the Maypole, eggs, and hares.
