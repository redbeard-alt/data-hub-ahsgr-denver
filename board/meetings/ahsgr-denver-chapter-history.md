# Denver Metro Chapter — AHSGR Chapter History

## About the Chapter

The Denver Metro Chapter is a regional chapter of the American Historical Society
of Germans from Russia (AHSGR), serving descendants of Germans who settled in
Russia — primarily in the Volga River and Black Sea regions — before emigrating
to the United States in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

The chapter is based in the Denver metro area and serves members throughout
Colorado, with approximately 78 active memberships as of June 2025.
Approximately 100 members have email addresses; an additional 100 members
receive the newsletter by postal mail only.

## Heritage Context

Germans from Russia are a distinct ethnic and cultural group: ethnic Germans
who accepted Catherine the Great's invitation in the 1760s to settle the
Russian steppes, maintaining German language, Lutheran and Catholic faith,
and distinct folk traditions for over a century before emigrating — primarily
to the Great Plains and Mountain West of the United States.

Key regional subgroups relevant to chapter members:
- **Volga Germans** — settled along the Volga River
- **Black Sea Germans** — settled in the Odessa/southern Ukraine region

AHSGR (ahsgr.org) is the national organization supporting research, genealogy,
and cultural preservation for this community.

**Why Lincoln, NE?** AHSGR originated in Colorado. The Amon family donated land in Lincoln,
NE to the organization on the condition that it be used as national headquarters — that is
why the national HQ is in Lincoln rather than Denver. (Source: Shirley Wilcoxon interview,
May 2026)

## Chapter Meeting History

- **Hampton Ave & Colorado Blvd church** — long-time home for chapter meetings and biannual
  German food events ("barracks"). Rental costs eventually reached $400/meeting (room) or
  $1,000+ (room + kitchen), forcing the chapter to relocate.
- **Shirley's home** — chapter shifted to hosted meetings after the church became too expensive;
  events became indoor picnics and Harvest Fest (rebranded from Oktoberfest).
- **12110 N Pecos St, Westminster** (Coldwell Banker Realty) — 2026 Maifest Potluck location.
- **Troy Younger** (Volga German, Commerce City) — catered chapter events during the Hampton
  Ave era.
- Denver chapter donated **30+ boxes of historical materials** to the Lincoln national headquarters.
